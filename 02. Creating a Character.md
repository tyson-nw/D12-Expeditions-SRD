The character is the vessel through which the Player experiences the game. the following sections will guide you though the process.

# Steps to create a character

1. Envision the character you want to play and write you character's [[Glossary#Personality Trait|Personality Traits]].
2. Choose the character's [[Glossary#Culture]]. Cultures are also a form of [[Glossary#Expertise]]. Additionally, cultures provide one [[Glossary#Feature]].
3. Choose the character's [[Glossary#Background|Backgrounds]]. Backgrounds are also a form of [[Glossary#Expertise]]. They provide the Character's starting equipment and may also include a [[Glossary#Feature]].
4. Choose the character's [[Glossary#Ancestry|Ancestries]]. Ancestries provide two [[Glossary#Feature|Features]]. Characters of mixed ancestry take one feature from each of their two dominant ancestries.
5. Choose the character's [[Glossary#Archetype|Archetypes]].
6. The Archetype gives you more equipment, advises you on your primary [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]], provides your base [[Glossary#HP]], and grants [[Glossary#Skill|Skills]].
7. Assign the array of stats +2, +2, +1, +1, +0, -1 and then increase one stat by +1.
8. Choose your skills. The [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 [[Glossary#Proficiency Bonus]] is +1.
9. Determine your defenses:
    - [[Glossary#Body]] is [[Glossary#Strength]] + [[Glossary#Fortitude]]
    - [[Glossary#React]] is [[Glossary#Dexterity]] + [[Glossary#Wits]]
    - [[Glossary#Mind]] is [[Glossary#Aptitude]] + [[Glossary#Will]]
10. Look up armor and weapon details.
11. Derive your stats
    - [[Glossary#Deflect]] is [[Glossary#React]] defense + any armor or shield bonus.
    - [[Glossary#Soak]] is [[Glossary#Fortitude]] bonus + any armor bonus.
    - [[Glossary#Maximum HP]] is Set by the Archetype's [[Glossary#Base HP]] plus [[Glossary#Fortitude]].
12. Write out your Character's [[Glossary#Action|Actions]], [[Glossary#Maneuver|Maneuvers]], [[Glossary#Response|Responses]], [[Glossary#Interation|Interactions]], [[Glossary#Response|Responses]] and [[Glossary#Perk|Perks]]
13. Purchase any additional [[Glossary#Equipment]].

# Personality Traits

By playing a character's personality traits, the Game Master rewards [[Glossary#Reroll|Rerolls]] that can be spent later. When the Character is embodying the trait, the Player can make and aside comment indicating that they are playing to the Character's [[Glossary#Drive]], [[Glossary#Flaw]], [[Glossary#Fear]].

## [[Glossary#Drive]]

What drives the Character? Why do they adventure? Why didn't they just take the same job as a parent and stay near their home? They should be active goals that are not permanently achievable but can be acted on in the moment.

- To protect those who cannot protect themselves.
- To break the bonds of tyranny.
- To live free and experience the world.
- To repay a debt I can never repay.
- To seek wealth and the pleasures it brings.

## [[Glossary#Flaw]]

What undermines the character? Is this the Character's drive taken to extreme? Is it a weakness that the Character will revert to when pressed? Acting on the Character's Flaw is the easiest to gain [[Glossary#Reroll|Rerolls]]. As well, overcoming the flaw at great cost to the character is worth gaining [[Glossary#Reroll|Rerolls]].

- I am a braggart that will bite off more than I can chew.
- I care more about enriching myself than the lives of others.
- In the face of overwhelming odds I will abandon my fellows.
- The pursuit of knowledge blinds me to the plight of others.

## [[Glossary#Fear]]
What does the Character fear? What will dissuade them from their goal? What holds them back and betray their oaths? This should be an existential fear, not of a physical object they can encounter.

- To die alone and unremarked.
- To be hated or reviled.
- To lose those I’ve sworn to protect.
- To be betrayed by those I trusted.

# [[Glossary#Ancestry|Ancestries]]

What bloodline does the character come from? What [[Glossary#Ancestry]] [[Glossary#Feature|Features]] does that bloodline bestow? A full-blooded character gains both Ancestry [[Glossary#Feature|Features]] (unless Small Sized, then they gain three). A mixed-blooded character chooses one [[Glossary#Feature]] from each parent's ancestry. If one of the Ancestries includes Small Sized the Player chooses whether it applies.

### Dwarf Blooded

- **Low Light Vision** Dwarves can see in [[Glossary#Dim Light]] as if it were [[Glossary#Bright Light]].
- **Stout Physique** Dwarves have [[Glossary#Edge]] on [[Glossary#Body]] Rolls.

### Elf Blooded

- **Low Light Vision** Elves can see in [[Glossary#Dim Light]] as if it were [[Glossary#Bright Light]].
- **Keen Senses** A D4 is added to [[Glossary#Senses]] Rolls.

### Gnome Blooded

- **Mechanical Mind** Gnomes have [[Glossary#Edge]] on [[Glossary#Mind]] Rolls.
- **Low Light Vision** Gnomes can see in [[Glossary#Dim Light]] as if it were [[Glossary#Bright Light]].
- **Small Stature** Small creatures can [[Glossary#Stride]] 5 spaces. Small creatures gain [[Glossary#Full Cover]] when standing behind a [[Glossary#Medium]] sized or larger creature.

### Halfun Blooded

- **Luck** As a [[Glossary#Response]] a Halfun can add an additional D4 to any Roll.
- **Vanish** Halfuns add a D4 to their [[Glossary#Stealth]] Rolls.
- **Small Stature** Small creatures can [[Glossary#Stride]] 5 spaces. Small creatures gain [[Glossary#Full Cover]] when standing behind a [[Glossary#Medium]] sized or larger creature.

### Human blooded

- **Coordination** Humans can move half their [[Glossary#Stride]] when performing a [[Glossary#Maneuver]] that doesn't include movement.
- **Recuperative** When recovering [[Glossary#HP]], humans recover an additional 1d4 HP.

### Orc Blooded

- **Relentless** When reduced to 0 [[Glossary#HP|HP]] an orc is reduced to 1 [[Glossary#HP]] instead. This ability can be used once per [[Glossary#Long Rest]] .
- **Overwhelming Blows** As a [[Glossary#Weapons]] roll [[Glossary#Perk]]  , add their [[Glossary#Body|Body]]to damage.

# [[Glossary#Culture|Cultures]] 

A character is largely defined by the cultural milieu in which they were brought up. The culture imparts values, incentives, and expectations to the character. Mechanically this can be added to a roll as an [[Glossary#Expertise]] where the character’s culture would impart that knowledge or skills. As well, each culture can bestow a [[Glossary#Feature|Features]] or more specified [[Glossary#Expertise]].

### Academic Citadel

The character was raised and educated in a citadel dedicated to the preservation of knowledge.

- **Arcane Knack** The character knows the [[Enigmas#Dancing Light]] or [[Enigmas#Prestidigitation]] cantrip. If the character doesn't have a [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Roll]] this Enigma has a [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Roll]] of [[Glossary#Aptitude]] + [[Glossary#Knowledge]] .

### Cloistered

The character grows up in a closed community, possibly religious but not necessarily. It had limited contact with outsiders. The community has a society that represents it in the wider world.

- **Secret Knowledge** The character knows the ins and outs of the society that they were cloistered with. Its rituals, its hierarchy, its secret ways. They also gain an [[Glossary#Expertise]] in Arcana, History, Religion, or other appropriate specialized knowledge of their choice.

### Fortress Mine

The character was raised in mining settlements within a mountain.

- **Stonereading** The character has [[Glossary#Expertise]] when on Rolls involving stonework, its creation, destruction, or history.

### Sylvan Glade

The character was raised in harmony with nature within the woods.

- **Skilled Hunter** The character has [[Glossary#Expertise]] in bows.

### Nomadic

The character was raised on the move.

- **Beastmaster** The character is adept in the ways of training [[Glossary#Beast|Beasts]] and commanding them. Successes of Training Rolls to train a [[Glossary#Beast]] count as two successes, Failure does not take away a Success. And the character has Expertise in riding, and maintaining [[Glossary#Beast|Beasts]] 

### Outlands

The character grew up in the wilds.

- **Wilderness Survival** The character has [[Glossary#Expertise]] in living off the land and knowing the cycles of nature.

### Rural

The character grew up in a rural environment. They are experienced in subsistence farming and all the tasks required to survive.

- **Disarming Personality** The character has a down home charm that gives them [[Glossary#Expertise]] on convincing NPC's of a character's friendliness and honesty.

### Urban

The character grew up in an urban center.

- **One of the Crowd** The character knows how to move through occupied spaces without penalty, and has Expertise in urban life.

# [[Glossary#Archetype|Archetypes]] 

Each character fits within an archetype that informs what the character can do. They advise what [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] the Archetype uses, provide [[Glossary#Skill]] proficiencies, the Character's [[Glossary#Base HP]], starting equipment and [[Glossary#Feature|Features]]. As the character advances their Archetype provides more [[Glossary#Feature|Features]].

### Adherent

Adherents are called by the divine to act in the world and channel their power. Their favored [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] are [[Glossary#Aptitude]]and [[Glossary#Will]]. They are proficient in [[Glossary#Enigmas]] , [[Glossary#Knowledge]] , [[Glossary#Social]] , and one additional [[Glossary#Skill]] . Adherents have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 8. They begin with a [[Glossary#Basic]] weapon, Holy Symbol, and a Chain Shirt.

  

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Divine Conduit

Adherents channel Enigmas from a [[Glossary#Divinity]] . Their [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Bonus]] is [[Glossary#Will]] + [[Glossary#Enigmas]], they use a Holy Symbol as a [[Glossary#Foci]], and do not need a free hand to cast Enigmas. They have access to a number of Enigmas as their [[Glossary#Aptitude]] +[[Glossary#Knowledge]] to a minimum of one and two [[Glossary#Cantrip|Cantrips]] . As a Downtime Action they can pray to be granted a different selection of [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 Enigmas. They have a base [[Glossary#Burn]] of 6 that resets after a [[Glossary#Long Rest]] .

##### Domain

Adherents also have specific Enigmas granted to them by their [[Glossary#Divinity]] . Every [[Glossary#Divinity]] has one or more domains that they have dominion over. Adherents channel their power through one of their [[Glossary#Divinity]] 's Domains. The Adherent always has the Domain Enigma prepared and they don't count against the Adherent's list of prepared Enigmas.

### Artificer

Artificers merge mechanical, chemical, and magical arts to create mechanisms. Their favored stats are Aptitude and Wits. They are proficient in Craft, Knowledge, Weapons, and one additional Skill. Artificers have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 6. They begin with a multitool, a codex, a Tinker's Parts kit, and a weapon.

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Multitool

The Artificer has a multitool that they built, it acts as Tinkerer's Tools. During a Short or [[Glossary#Long Rest]] they can adjust it to contain one cantrip from their Codex. The [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Roll]] of Enigmas cast through the Multitool is [[Glossary#Wits]] + [[Glossary#Weapons]] . Rolling a 1 on the Enigmacasting Roll disables the Multitool until repaired during a Short Rest.

##### Manual

The Artificer uses a Codex to contain their formula and blueprints for their constructions. They start with two Cantrips and as many [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 Enigmas as their Aptitude. They can scribe a new Enigma or mechanism with a number of Downtime actions based on the [[Glossary#Tier]] of the Enigma. An Artificer can only transcribe a Enigma of the Artificer's current [[Glossary#Tier]] or lower.

##### Empower Charge

During a [[Glossary#Long Rest]] the Artificer can craft 2 + [[Glossary#Aptitude]]ammunition worth of [[Glossary#Tier]] ed Enigmas. These Enigmas can be channeled through the Artificer's multitool to cast. These charges fade when the Artificer takes a [[Glossary#Long Rest]]. When casting a Enigma as ammunition, as a Perk the ammunition is not expended.

### Bard

Bards embolden their allies, plague their enemies, and shape arcane power. Their favored stats are [[Glossary#Wits]] and [[Glossary#Will]]. They are proficient in [[Glossary#Focus]], [[Glossary#Knowledge]] , [[Glossary#Social]] and two additional skills. Bards have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 8. Bard begin with a weapon with the [[Glossary#Basic]] [[Glossary#Tag|Tags]], and an instrument.

  

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Arcane Melody

The Bard channels the power of music to manipulate the world. Their [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Roll]] is [[Glossary#Will]] + [[Glossary#Knowledge]] and uses their instrument as a [[Glossary#Foci]] be able to speak and not be silenced. They know as many [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 Enigmas as their [[Glossary#Wits]] + [[Glossary#Knowledge]] to a minimum of 1. To cast a Enigma they spend twice the Enigma's [[Glossary#Tier]] worth of Fervor.

##### Battle Fervor

The Bard gains 1 Fervor when the target of their Exhortation [[Glossary#Exploit|Exploits]] a foe. The Bard has 2 Fervor after a [[Glossary#Short Rest]].

##### Exhortation

As a [[Glossary#Maneuver]] the Bard designates an ally that can hear them, that ally adds a 1d4 to their next Roll.

##### Wounding Wit

As an [[Glossary#Action]] the Bard makes a [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Roll]] against a target's [[Glossary#Mind|Mind]] defense that can hear them and deals 1d4 damage per [[Glossary#Tier]] of the Bard.

### Berserker

Berserkers channel their primal rage into strength of arms. Their favored [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] are [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] and [[Glossary#Strength]]. They are proficient in [[Glossary#Athletics]], [[Glossary#Focus]], [[Glossary#Weapons]] , and one additional [[Glossary#Skill]] . Berserkers have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 12, and begin with a [[Glossary#Martial]] weapon and two [[Glossary#Thrown]] weapons.

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Rage

Berserkers have a base [[Glossary#Burn]] of 6 and can fly into a rage with [[Glossary#Interaction]] by making [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] + [[Glossary#Focus]] against their current [[Glossary#Burn]] . If they took damage last round they have an [[Glossary#Edge]] on the roll. When they rage, increase their [[Glossary#Burn]] by one. While in a Rage the Berserker adds their [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] to damage, and does not go [[Glossary#Unconscious]] when reduced to 0 [[Glossary#HP|HP]] but are [[Glossary#Dying]]. The Rage lasts until [[Glossary#Initiative]]ends or they decide to end it.

##### Fearsome

With a [[Glossary#Maneuver]] the Berserker can intimidate someone within a 6 space [[Glossary#Range]]with a [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] + [[Glossary#Social]] roll verses the target's [[Glossary#Mind]] defense to make them [[Glossary#Frightened]] until the end of their next turn.

### Dedicant

Dedicants are dedicated to an oath and through that oath they express their life. Their favored [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] are [[Glossary#Strength]]and [[Glossary#Will]]. They are proficient in [[Glossary#Athletics]], [[Glossary#Focus]], [[Glossary#Weapons]] , and one additional [[Glossary#Skill]] . Dedicants have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 10 and begin with a [[Glossary#Martial]] weapon and Chainmail.

  

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Channel Oath

Dedicant channel the power of their oath to shape the world. Their [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Bonus]] is [[Glossary#Will]] + [[Glossary#Focus]], they can use their weapon as a [[Glossary#Foci]] (or a holy symbol if their oath is religious), and do not need a free hand to cast Enigmas. They know as many [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 Enigmas as their [[Glossary#Will]] + [[Glossary#Focus]] to a minimum of one. Their Fervor resets to 2 after a [[Glossary#Short Rest]]. To cast a Enigma they spend twice the Enigma's [[Glossary#Tier]] worth of Fervor.

##### Rebuke

As a [[Glossary#Response]] to an enemy attacking the Dedicant or an adjacent ally, the Dedicant can make a weapon attack against the attacker that deals damage as normal and [[Glossary#Expose|Exposes]] the target. If the damage is greater than the Attack's target number the attack fails.

##### Rising Fervor

The Dedicant gains Fervor each time the Dedicant [[Glossary#Exploit|Exploits]] a foe.

##### Smite

As an [[Glossary#Interaction]], the Dedicant can spend 1 [[Glossary#Fervor]] to do 1d8 damage to the target of the Dedicant's previous weapon attack.

### Primal Shaper

Primalshapers are one with nature. Their favored [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] are [[Glossary#Will]] and [[Glossary#Wits]], they are proficient in [[Glossary#Enigmas]], [[Glossary#Knowledge]], [[Glossary#Focus]], and one additional [[Glossary#Skill]] . Primalshapers have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 8. Primalshapers begin with a [[Glossary#Basic]] weapon, leather armor, and a Primal Foci they created.

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Primal Channel

Enigmas are revealed to Primalshapers through their connection with the primal forces of nature. Their [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Bonus]] is [[Glossary#Will]] + [[Glossary#Enigmas]] and they use a Primalshaper Foci or Apothecary Pouch as a [[Glossary#Foci]] . They know as many [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 Enigmas as their [[Glossary#Wits]] + [[Glossary#Knowledge]] and two [[Glossary#Cantrip|Cantrips]]. During a [[Glossary#Long Rest]] they can commune with nature and change the Primalshaper Enigmas they know. They have a starting [[Glossary#Burn]] of 6 that resets after a [[Glossary#Long Rest]].

##### Wild Shape

As a [[Glossary#Maneuver]] a Primalshaper can roll against their [[Glossary#Burn]] to polymorph into one of two [[Glossary#Beast]] of Challenge 2 or lower that the Wildeshaper knows. Communing with nature as a  [[Glossary#Downtime]]action they can change what beasts they can polymorph into. They gain the beast's [[Glossary#HP|HP]] in [[Glossary#Temporary HP]] along with its [[Glossary#Strength]], [[Glossary#Fortitude]], and [[Glossary#Dexterity]] bonuses. Their Defenses, [[Glossary#Deflect]] and [[Glossary#Soak]] are derived from these new bonuses. When the Wild Shaped Primalshaper's [[Glossary#Temporary HP]] is reduced to 0 they revert to their natural form and take any additional damage left over from the attack. Wildshaping increases their [[Glossary#Burn]] by 2. The Primalshaper has two shapes they can wildshape into.

### Rogue

Rogues are always watching for opportunities they can exploit. Their favored [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] are [[Glossary#Dexterity|Dexterity]] and [[Glossary#Wits]]. They are proficient in [[Glossary#Athletics]], [[Glossary#Weapons]] , [[Glossary#Social]] , [[Glossary#Stealth]], and two other skills. Rogues have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 8. Rogues begin with a Shortsword, a Dagger, and Leather Armor.

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Stealthy

As a [[Glossary#Maneuver]] a Rogue can  [[Glossary#Hide]] and move their [[Glossary#Stride]] speed.

##### Sneak Attack 2d6

As an [[Glossary#Action]], [[Glossary#Exploit]] a foe by making a weapon attack with a [[Glossary#Fine]] weapon to deal weapon damage and an additional 2 weapon damage die.

### Scout

Scouts are masters of the wilds. Their favored [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] are [[Glossary#Dexterity|Dexterity]] and [[Glossary#Wits]]. They are proficient in, [[Glossary#Senses]], [[Glossary#Stealth]], [[Glossary#Weapons]] and one other skill. Scouts have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 10. They begin with a Scimitar, a Longbow with a quiver of 20 arrows, and Leather Armor.

  

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Foebinding

As an [[Glossary#Maneuver]] a Scout can designate a foe. As long as they make no attacks against other foes or make responses to other foes, they have an additional 1d4 on [[Glossary#Weapons]] and [[Glossary#Senses]] rolls against that foe.

##### Wildswalker

A Scout can move through  [[Glossary#Difficult Terrain]] and  [[Glossary#Hazardous Terrain]] without a penalty to their speed and can move at full speed while hidden.

##### Wildcrafting

A Scout has [[Glossary#Expertise]] in tracking, hunting, navigating, and wilderness survival.

### Sorcerer

Sorcerers draw on their inborn power to craft Enigmas. Their favored [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] are [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] and [[Glossary#Aptitude]] . They are proficient in [[Glossary#Focus]], [[Glossary#Senses]], and two additional [[Glossary#Skill]] s. Sorcerers have a [[Glossary#Base HP]] of 6.

#### [[Glossary#Tier]] 1

##### Arcane Pool

The sorcerer has a Pool of Sorcery Points equal to their [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] + [[Glossary#Focus]] that they spend to cast Enigmas. They regain their Sorcery Points during a [[Glossary#Short Rest]]. Casting a Enigma requires the Enigma's [[Glossary#Tier]] in Enigma points. The Sorcerer can cast [[Glossary#Cantrip|Cantrips]] as long as they have at least one Enigma Point. Their [[Glossary#Enigmacasting Roll]] is [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] + [[Glossary#Focus]]. Casting a [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 Enigma requires a [[Glossary#Moderate]] Enigmacasting Roll. As a [[Glossary#Perk]], the Enigma costs no Sorcery Points.

##### Arcane Sense

The Sorcerer can sense ongoing Enigmas, know what Enigma is being cast by a creature, or see the remnants of Enigmas that were cast within 1 hour for every [[Glossary#Tier]] the caster is.

##### Blood Knowledge

The Sorcerer knows two Cantrips and their [[Glossary#Aptitude]] +[[Glossary#Focus]] in [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 Sorcerer Enigmas.

##### Channel Blood

The Sorcerer can cast a Enigma at one higher [[Glossary#Tier]] than the Sorcerer is by spending twice the sorcery points of the [[Glossary#Tier]] of the Enigma.

# Assigning Stats

Distribute the following bonuses +2, +2, +1, +1, 0, -1, one to each stat as best fits the character. Once the Stats have been assigned increase one Stat by +1.

## [[Glossary#Derived Stat|Derived Stats]]

Derived Stats are calculated from a character's normal stats to round them out.

### [[Glossary#Defense|Defenses]]

There are Four Defenses, each derived from a combination of two [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]] .

#### [[Glossary#Body]]

[[Glossary#Strength|Strength]]and [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] combine to make up a creature's Body defense. This is used to resist poison and other attacks on the creature's health.

#### [[Glossary#React]]

[[Glossary#Dexterity|Dexterity]] and [[Glossary#Wits]] combine to make up a creature's React defense. This is used to avoid attacks or other hazards.

#### [[Glossary#Mind]]

[[Glossary#Aptitude]]and [[Glossary#Will]] combine to make up a creature's Mind defense. This is used to resist attempts to bend their will, confuse them, or otherwise attack the creatures psyche.

### [[Glossary#Deflect]]

A character's Deflect is their [[Glossary#React|React]]defense plus any [[Glossary#Deflection Bonus]] provided by the armor or shield they are wearing.

### [[Glossary#Soak]]

A character's soak is their [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] + the [[Glossary#Soak]] granted by their worn armor or shield. It is used to reduce the amount of damage taken, to a minimum of 1 damage.

### [[Glossary#HP]]

HP, or hit points, is an abstraction of how many times a character can be hit. Of a character drops to 0 HP they are [[Glossary#Unconscious]] and begin [[Glossary#Dying]].

#### [[Glossary#Current HP]]

The creature's current HP. It can only be equal to or lesser than the Maximum HP, even if healing would increase it further. Once they fall to 0 [[Glossary#HP|HP]] or below they begin dying.

#### [[Glossary#Maximum HP]]

The maximum HP a creature can have. A character's [[Glossary#Base HP]] is set by their class plus their [[Glossary#Fortitude]] bonus multiplied by the character's [[Glossary#Proficiency Bonus]].

####  [[Glossary#Temporary HP]]

Temporary HP are additional beyond the character's HP. When a character gains Temporary HP they replace the current value if the new value is higher. When the character takes damage the Temporary HP goes away first. [[Glossary#Dying]] creatures with Temporary HP are still [[Glossary#Dying]] until they stabilize. If a creature has Temporary HP they are not [[Glossary#Unconscious]] from having 0 HP.

### [[Glossary#Proficiency Bonus]]

This is determined by the [[Glossary#Tier]] of play. [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 characters have a proficiency bonus of +1 and higher [[Glossary#Tier|Tiers]] have correspondingly higher bonuses.

### Tags

Tags are aspects of the weapon that detail its use, grant [[Glossary#Feature|Features]], or otherwise effect how they are used.

#### [[Glossary#Ammunition]]

Weapon uses ammunition that is tracked separately and may add damage dealt or effects granted by the weapon. Ammunition breaks on misses and cannot be recovered.

#### [[Glossary#Basic]]

Does not require proficiency in [[Glossary#Weapons]] to use without [[Glossary#Hindrance]].

#### [[Glossary#Bladed]]

Deals damage by cutting a target. As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   the target gains the [[Glossary#Bleeding]] condition

#### [[Glossary#Blunt]]

Blunt weapons rely on impact to do damage. The wielder may choose to do non-lethal damage when they hit. If the attack would drop the target to 0 [[Glossary#HP|HP]] or below, they have 1 [[Glossary#HP|HP]] and are [[Glossary#Unconscious]]. As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   the wielder choses which of the target's stats to [[Glossary#Blight]]  .

#### [[Glossary#Charging]]

If the wielder moves at least half their speed before striking, deal an additional die of damage. As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   the attack knocks the target [[Glossary#Prone]]  .

#### [[Glossary#Disarming]]

On a hit if the attack roll also exceeds the target's [[Glossary#Body|Body]]defense the attacker can choose to remove an item being held by the target instead of damage. As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   the target is [[Glossary#Expose]].

#### [[Glossary#Emplaced]]

These are non-portable weapons that require concerted effort beyond that of an individual to move or are built into a location or vehicle. Emplaced weapons use [[Glossary#Wits]] for Rolls and dealing damage.

#### [[Glossary#Finesse]]

Can use [[Glossary#Dexterity]] instead of [[Glossary#Strength]]when making an attack.

####  [[Glossary#Fine]]

Can be used to attack as a [[Glossary#Maneuver]] in the off-hand.

#### [[Glossary#Heavy]]

Requires a [[Glossary#Strength]]modifier of +2, +3 for creatures with small stature. Otherwise, the wielder has a [[Glossary#Hindrance]] on attack rolls. As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   instead of rolling for damage, the attack deals maximum damage for the weapon.

#### [[Glossary#Heavy Armor]]

Requires a Strength of +3 to wear. Otherwise the wearer has a [[Glossary#Hindrance]] on attack and [[Glossary#React]] Rolls.

#### [[Glossary#Light Armor]]

Simple armor with no requirements.

#### [[Glossary#Martial]]

Requires [[Glossary#Weapons]] [[Glossary#Skill]] otherwise the wielder has a [[Glossary#Hindrance]] on attack, [[Glossary#Deflect]], and [[Glossary#React|]] Rolls.

#### [[Glossary#Mechanical]]

Requires a [[Glossary#Maneuver]] to reload. Uses [[Glossary#Wits]] for rolls and damage bonus.

#### [[Glossary#Medium Armor]]

Requires a Strength of +1 to wear, otherwise the wearer has a [[Glossary#Hindrance]] on [[Glossary#React]]rolls.

#### [[Glossary#Mounted]]

Can be used when mounted even if other [[Glossary#Tag|Tags]] would prevent it.

#### [[Glossary#Natural]]

When a creature uses its body to deal damage they are using a natural weapon. Natural weapons use [[Glossary#Strength]] +[[Glossary#Athletics]] to hit and deal [[Glossary#Strength]]in damage. If the attack doesn't have a damage die, they just deal the creature's [[Glossary#Strength]]stat for damage. A successful attempt to disarm a natural weapon disables the natural weapon until the the creature recovers all their [[Glossary#HP]]. As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   the target is Grabbed.

#### [[Glossary#Noisy]]

If Armor, the wearer has a [[Glossary#Hindrance]] on [[Glossary#Stealth]] rolls while wearing it. If a Weapon, the wielder is not longer hidden to creatures who can hear when used.

#### [[Glossary#Piercing]]

Deals damage by piercing the target. As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   add bypass target's [[Glossary#Soak]].

#### [[Glossary#Ranged]]

Can be fired at a single target up to the listed distance or beyond the listed distance up to three times the listed distance with a [[Glossary#Hindrance]]. Uses [[Glossary#Dexterity]] for rolls and bonus damage. If the attacker is adjacent to an enemy they have a [[Glossary#Hindrance]].

#### [[Glossary#Reach]]

Can strike targets up to 2 spaces away, unless otherwise stated.

#### [[Glossary#Basic]]

Does not require proficiency in [[Glossary#Weapons]] to use without [[Glossary#Hindrance]].

#### [[Glossary#Shield]]

Can be used in an [[Glossary#Offhand Attack]] to deal 1d4 damage. As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   for an defense the wielder can [[Glossary#Push]] attacker back 2 spaces.

#### [[Glossary#Slam]]

As a [[Glossary#Perk]]   the wielder can choose to push the target up to 2 spaces and [[Glossary#Expose]] them.

#### [[Glossary#Thrown]]

Can make an attack against a target within the listed distance using a [[Glossary#Strength]] + [[Glossary#Weapons]] Roll. Can be thrown at a target up to twice times the listed distance with [[Glossary#Hindrance]].

#### [[Glossary#Two-Handed]]

Requires two hands to wield. Wielders have an [[Glossary#Edge]] to resist disarming.

#### [[Glossary#Versatile]]

Can be wielded two-handed or single-handed in melee. When wielded two-handed it uses the listed die to deal damage instead of normal damage.

### Arcane Foci

These [[Glossary#Foci]] are used to cast a [[Glossary#Enigma]]. They can be Amulets, Crystals, Gloves, Knives, Orbs, Rods, Staffs, and Wands or other thematically appropriate form.

### Codex, 100 pages

Codices are specially treated for durability and use in the field. Each one has 100 pages that can contain Alchemical Formulae, Crafting Recipes, Enigmas, and other knowledge.

### Healers Kit

Healers Kits have 10 charges. Expending a charge the Healers kit grant an [[Glossary#Edge]] when healing damage, either mundanely or magically. As an Action and expending a charge the healers kit can be used to end all [[Glossary#Bleeding]]   conditions. Expending two charges makes healing dice roll their maximum.

### Holy Symbol

Holy Symbols of a God are ues to channel a [[Glossary#Enigma]] from a Divinity. They are generally amulets, sigils, censors, or rods. Shields and Weapons can have a holy symbol mounted on them.

### Primal Foci

These are living plants that are cultivated by the caster to tap into primal powers. They can take the form of amulet, sprig, and staff or other thematically appropriate form. Cultivating and repairing damage to them can be done during a [[Glossary#Long Rest]] without strenuous activity. As a  [[Glossary#Downtime]]action the foci can be created with a Moderate Complex Roll of [[Glossary#Wits]] + [[Glossary#Knowledge]] to gather supplies, [[Glossary#Will]] + [[Glossary#Focus]] to cultivate it, and [[Glossary#Will]] + [[Glossary#Enigmas]] to empower the foci.

### Thieves Tools

Thieves tools are required to make Rolls to open locks and to disable devices. They contain lockpicks, oils, a crowbar, and lengths of thin rope.

### Tinker's Parts

This kit contains parts useful to create mechanisms. It contains 40 charges worth of parts that can be used to create mechanisms.

## Crafting Tools

Crafting tools are a portable kit of tools that a character will use to make Craft Rolls. Without an appropriate kit the character has a Hinderance on their Rolls. Not all craft rolls can be achieved with just crafting tools but require a full workshop.

- **Alchemist Tools** Used for crafting Alchemical Items
- **Carpenter Tools** Used for woodworking
- **Herbalism Tools** Used for gathering and preserving reagents and components.
- **Jeweler Tools** Used for crafting fine items or Enchanted Items.
- **Masonry Tools** used for working stone
- **Tinkerer Tools** Used for creating, modifying, and disabling mechanisms and traps.
- **Trapsmith Tools** Used for creating snares and simple traps.

## Pets

Pets act on their master’s turn and are trained to move where directed,  [[Glossary#Hide]], or [[Glossary#Dodge]] in combat. Commanding a pet is a [[Glossary#Maneuver]] . If attacked, and not given instructions otherwise, the pet will attack its attacker. Instead of being purchased they can be trained using [[Glossary#Downtime|Downtimes]].

> ##### Training Pets for Combat
> Training a pet for combat takes time and effort. To purchase one multiply the cost of the animal by 10. Once the pet is trained, it can be trained for combat.
