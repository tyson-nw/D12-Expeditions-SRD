Foes are Defined not by their nature or behavior, but by their Conflict with the Characters. There is no consistent moral, ethical, or political alignment between Foes and each has their own Motivations. How those Motivations conflict with the Characters' is the source of Conflict.

# Motivations

The most important aspect of a foe is their Motivation, be it hunger, greed, malice, or simple disagreement. Their Motivation dictates why they are hostile to the characters, how far they are willing to go, and what they want out of the Conflict.

# Combat 

When Conflicts raise to violence Foes are armed with Stats, Features, and Moves similar to face Characters with.

## Foe Stats

### Size

How much space a Creature takes up and needs to combat. Not only does it effect the size of their tokens, it effects the spaces they Threaten.

### Types

Creatures are distributed into broad categories. They may also have an Ancestry they belong to within that category.

#### Beast 

Creatures of the natural world are beasts.

#### Construct

Creature that have been built and animates through arcane means.

#### Dragon 

Great creatures with a draconic lineage.

#### Giants

Creatures that tower over lesser beings.

#### Humanoid

Creatures that have the humanesqe form that are small or medium sized.

#### Monstrosity

Creatures that have been created by magic or beasts twisted into a monstrous form.

#### Outsider

Creatures native to a plane other than the Material Plane. They are aligned with one of the cosmic forces of Law or Chaos.

- **Divine** Typically agents of the forces of Law or Gods.
- **Infernal** Typically agents of the forces of Chaos or the lower planes.
- **Elemental** Tied to an elemental plane.
- **Fey** Tied to the Fey Realm
- **Aberration** Something from beyond the multiverse.

#### Plant

Creatures that are plants, fungus, or derived from plants or fungus.

#### Undead

Creatures that have been reanimated by negative energy.

### Challenge

The challenge the relative difficulty of the creature. It is a determined by a combination of Defenses, [[Glossary#HP]], Features and Attacks per round.

### Defenses

Creatures don't roll Defenses, they have a set [[Glossary#Target Number]] to be overcome.

### Derived Stats

As with Defenses, the Derived Stats are a static number that may or may not directly relate to the Stats they are Derived from.

### Features

Passive features that fall outside the standard Moves are Features.

#### Skills

If a Defense is not appropriate for the Character to Roll against, a creature my have a Skill that is rolled against instead.

## Features

	##### Attack Complications
	If a Character Rolls a Complication when targeting a creature, if the creature Threatens the Character, the Defense Complication applies.

## Moves

All creatures have access to the same Moves a Character has in Combat with one exception, the Lair action.
	
### Actions and Maneuvers

Each action that targets an enemy has a Target Number, the Defense the target Rolls, an outcome, and a complication for when the target gets a Complication on their Defense roll. 

#### Recharge

Some abilities need to recharge between uses. The Game Master rolls a D6 at the beginning of the creature's turn to determine if the ability recharges.

##### Weapon Complications
	If the move uses a weapon, use the weapon's Perk from its Tags as the complication.

#### Movement

There are a variety of modes of movement that can be used as a Maneuver.

##### Stride

Creatures can move up to their total stride spaces in one round. It is assumed that all creatures have a stride of 6 spaces unless otherwise stated. That movement can be interspersed with other actions as long as the total movement is no more than their movement speed. If a character rises from prone it uses half of the creature's speed.

A creature can move through another creature's space. When moving through an enemy’s space, the area is considered [[Glossary#Difficult Terrain]]. If a creature ends their turn within another creature's space they are [[[Glossary#Sharing a Space]].

##### Climb

Can move up and down steep or vertical surfaces without a penalty to movement.

##### Fly

The creature must move at least half their fly movement every round. They can only move upward half their total move. If the creature is knocked [[Glossary#Prone]] they begin falling.

##### Hover

The creature can move in any direction or not at all. The creature is immune to the [[Glossary#Prone]] condition.

##### Swim

The creature can move this speed through water and typically can breath in water.


### Reactions

Foes may have more than one reaction available to them and they may be able to take more than one a Turn.

### Lair Actions

When in their Lair, certain creatures have access to Lair Actions that they can trigger during the Environmental Events phase of a Round.

## Calculating Difficulty

To determine the difficulty of an combat conflict you determine the Party Strength by totaling the Tiers of the characters. As this is a Tier 1 adventure the Party Strength equals the character count.

Each combat conflict has an Encounter Difficulty, and you add as many opponents so their total total Challenge close to but than the difficulty threshold.
