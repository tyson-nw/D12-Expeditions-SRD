## Action

Actions take the lion’s share of a Turn. They do the most, hit the hardest, or have the most spectacular effects.

### Adjacent

Creatures whose occupied spaces touch another creature’s occupied spaces are considered Adjacent.

# Aptitude

Aptitude is how well a creature learns and retains knowledge for later recall. As well, it measures how well the creature can apply its knowledge in the pursuit of a task.

### Assist

Without a roll, while threatening the same foe, a creature can help an ally's Roll as long as they are trained in the same Skill. The ally gains an Edge on their next Roll.

# Athletics

Athletics is the creature's physical prowess. Their ability to focus their strength, their ability to perform feats of acrobatics, and their ability to endure exertion. There are some specific ways these skills are used.

###### Aura

The listed Distance extends in all directions from the target’s space.

# Blinded

The creature cannot see and as such has a Hindrance on Deflect, Enigmas, React, Senses, Weapons Rolls and are Exposed. Other creatures have an Edge on Rolls that target the Blinded creature. 

# Blighted

Blight targets a single Stat for a number of levels. For Characters, Skill Rolls and Defense Rolls that include the Blighted Stat subtract a D4 from that roll for each level of Blight. For NPCs, every time a Character rolls against the Blighted Stat, add a D4 to the character's roll. If a creature has more blight in one Stat as 2 plus their Fortitude Stat they become Incapacitated.

# Bleeding

The creature loses one HP at the start of their turn until they are healed at least 1 HP. Bleeding can be applied multiple times, the damage increases by 1 each time. If the creature is at 0 HP they no longer have the bleeding condition but are dying as normal.

# Body

[[Glossary#Strength|Strength]]and [[Glossary#Fortitude|Fortitude]] combine to make up a creature's Body defense. This is used to resist poison and other attacks on the creature's health.

### Bounds

The available area the Chase takes place in can be limited to a set number of spaces at each Distance, limiting how many Creatures can share that Distance. This is represented by the amount of Lanes available on the chase map.

# Bright Light
Bright light is easy to see in and confers no cover to creatures within it.

# Burn
If the character uses [[Burn]], the [[Target Number]] is both the caster's current [[Burn]] and the target's defense.

# Burning

At the the beginning of the burning creature's turn they take 1d6 damage and are Exposed. To end Burning condition the creature or an adjacent creature can snuff the flames as a Maneuver or fully be immersed in water.



# **Burrow** 
Burrowing creatures can move through solid earth in any direction.

#### [[Glossary#Cantrip|Cantrips]]
Cantrips are simple spells that can be cast at any time a Creature meets the casting requirements. Cantrips scale as the caster advances in the tiers, and how they do so are listen in the spell description.

# Charmed

The charmed creature cannot attack the creature that charmed it. The charmed creature has a Hindrance on rolls that use Will or their Mind defense when interacting with the creature that charmed it. Charmed creatures are Exposed.

###### Circle

The radius of a circle extends around a point in the center of the origin space extending the Distance listed. Circle 0 only covers the space with the point in it.

# **Colossal** 
Colossal creatures are larger than a Battlemap and rarely do more than protrude onto it. Their individual features will describe the area they Threaten.

# **Common** 
Comfortable is the average lifestyle of an individual that has steady housing, easy access to food, and is able to regularly clean themselves and their clothes costing 100 Coin per week of Downtime to maintain. Social Rolls with Noble individuals have a Hindrance.

# Complex Rolls

There are times when a simple Roll isn’t enough to cover all the aspects of an action. In those cases the Players make Complex Rolls. These entail three different Rolls, each to achieve a different aspect of the action. Rolls either succeed if they meet or beat the Complex Roll’s Target Number, or Fail if they are below the Target Number. As a Perk the roll counts as 2 successes, and Complications count as 2 failures. Once all three Rolls have been made subtract the failures from the successes. 

• 3+ Successes, the action succeeds with a perk.
• 2 Successes, the action succeeds.
• 1 Success, the action succeeds with a complication.
• 0 or fewer Successes, action fails.

# Complication

Rolls that fail to beat the [[Glossary#Target Number]] achieve the desired outcome but something goes wrong to cause further problems. This should not negate the success, but cause additional problems the characters need to overcome.

A 12 on the D12 that doesn’t meet the [[Glossary#Target Number]] Succeeds with a Complication.

#### [[Concentration]]
A character can only maintain one Concentration spell at a time. If a character takes damage while Concentrating on a spell they need to make a Will + Focus Roll against a Target Number of 4 + the damage the spell does before applying Soak.

# Craft

Craft is a creature's understanding of how to make things and how to break things.

###### Cube

The effect fills an area where the Distance defines each side of the area encompassed in 3d space. 

# Current HP

The creature's current HP. It can only be equal to or lesser than the Maximum HP, even if healing would increase it further. Once they fall to 0 [[Glossary#HP|HP]] or below they begin dying.

# Damage

When dealing damage, the source of the damage has a damage die listed. Roll that and apply the target's Soak. Subtract the remaining damage from the target's Temporary HP then, if there is any damage remaining, the target's Current HP. If the attack deals more damage than the creature has Current HP, the creature's HP becomes 0 and they begin Dying.

### Dash

Without a roll, a creature can make a Stride Maneuver a second time.

# Darkness 
Creatures in darkness are in Full Cover and Blinded.

# Daylight
Daylight is natural light from the sun. Its Dim Light is still considered daylight.


# Deafened

The character cannot hear and as such as a Hindrance on React, Senses, and Social Rolls. Stealth rolls have Edge against deafened creatures. Deafened creatures are Exposed.

# Defending

When a Character is attacked or otherwise the target of a spell or Feature, the Player rolls a d12 and adds the appropriate Defense bonus against a Target Number set by the attack.

# Defensive Complication

There is no universal Defensive Complication as each NPC action will have a described complication.

# Defensive Perk

By default the attacker is Exposed until the beginning of their next turn. Your Character may have features that make additional options for Defensive Perks.

# Defensive Roll

When a Character is subject to some action or effect the Player makes a Defensive Roll of a D12 plus the appropriate Defense.

- If the roll is a 12 on the D12, the defense succeeds. If it also meets or beats the [[Glossary#Target Number]] the Character gains a [[Glossary#Defensive Perk]].
- It the Roll meets or beats the [[Glossary#Target Number]]  the defense succeeds.
- If the Roll is below the [[Glossary#Target Number]] the Defense fails.
-  If the Roll is a 1 on the D12, the Character gains a [[Glossary#Defensive Complication]].

# Deflect

A character's Deflect is their [[Glossary#React|React]]defense plus any [[Glossary#Deflection Bonus]] provided by the armor or shield they are wearing.

# Deflection Bonus

This bonus adds to the character's [[Glossary#React|React]]to derive the character's [[Glossary#Deflect|Deflect]]. If the Deflection Bonus has a * then it is used as the [[Glossary#Deflect|Deflect]] without the [[Glossary#React|React]]bonus added in.

### Demeanor

How the NPC relates to the Characters is their Demeanor. As the players interact they may change the Demeanor up and down the ranks of the following scale.


# Dexterity

Dexterity is how physically skilled a creature is. It is a measure of fine control and flexibility.

### Difficult Terrain

Moving through one space of Difficult Terrain requires two spaces of movement.

# Dim Light 
Low Light is difficult to see in. Targets in Low Light are Screened. Low light continues beyond the light source twice its spaces of Bright Light.

### Dodge

Without a roll, a creature has an Edge on all React and Deflect rolls until the beginning of their next turn.

# Downtime

When your character is not adventuring they have Downtime which is expressed in weeks. This is when they are out of Conflict, living their lives. Their actions during Downtime can trigger Conflicts which make them an excellent vehicle for discovering new Plot threads.

# Drive

What drives the Character? Why do they adventure? Why didn't they just take the same job as a parent and stay near their home? They should be active goals that are not permanently achievable but can be acted on in the moment.

### [[Duration]]
This describes how long a spell lasts. If there is no duration it begins and ends instantaneously. Spells end if the caster begins dying regardless of Duration.

## Dying

When a creature reaches 0 HP they fall Unconscious and begin dying.

# **Easy** 
Anyone trained in the task can successfully achieve it. The Target Number is 6.

# Edge
A Character rolls an additional D12 and takes the higher roll.

# Enigmas

Enigmas are the supernatural forces that shape the world. Skill in Enigmas is used to make most [[Glossary#Spellcasting Roll|Spellcasting Rolls]].

# Expertise

Expertises are additional training that a character has received. They are not tied to a specific [[Glossary#Skill]] or [[[Glossary#Stat]]. Only one Expertise can be added to a Roll if it applies to the task, adding the +2 to the roll. The creature does not need to be proficient in a skill to use an Expertise in a Roll.

## Exploit

Exploit Features require a target to be Exposed to use. A Character cannot Expose and Exploit the same target in the same Turn.

## Expose

Exposing a target makes them vulnerable to attacks that Exploit. Some Conditions Expose a creature to specific attackers.

# Failure

Total failure is rare but when it happens the character fails completely causing new problems for the characters beyond the null result.

### Falling

Every Round a creature can fall up to 20 spaces. For each space the creature falls that Round they take 1d6 of damage.

###### Fan

The area projects outward in a ninety degree fan who's origin is an adjacent square to the origin Creature.

# Fear
What does the Character fear? What will dissuade them from their goal? What holds them back and betray their oaths? This should be an existential fear, not of a physical object they can encounter.

# Features

Beyond the [[Glossary#Stat|Stats]], [[Glossary#Skill|Skills]], and [[Glossary#Expertise|Expertieses]] that make up the Character's capabilities, the Character has [[Glossary#Features]]. These are abilities with their own specific rules that provide new actions to the Player that widens the Character's capabilities.

### Flanking

When allied creatures are opposite of each other with the target in the middle, the target is Exposed to their flanking attackers.

# Flaw

What undermines the character? Is this the Character's drive taken to extreme? Is it a weakness that the Character will revert to when pressed? Acting on the Character's Flaw is the easiest to gain [[Glossary#Reroll|Rerolls]]. As well, overcoming the flaw at great cost to the character is worth gaining [[Glossary#Reroll|Rerolls]].

# **Fly** 
A flying creature can move in any direction during their Turn. must move at least half of the Fly distance during their turn or they are knocked Prone begin to fall.

### [[Foci]]
Foci are specially crafted items that can be used to channel an Archetype's spellcasting. Some are permanent items, some require maintenance, and others are consumable.

# Focus

Focus is used to focus on a task, ignoring pain, wounds, or other distractions.

## Forced Movement

A creature can be forced to move by a controlling creature. Moving over Difficult Terrain requires spending two spaces of movement for each space moved as normal. When moving through Hazardous Terrain the moved creature is affected as if they moved themselves.

# Fortitude

Fortitude is how durable a creature is. It is a measure of their health and overall durability.

# Frightened

The creature cannot move towards the source of their fear. Frightened characters have a Hindrance on all rolls while they can sense the source of their fear. Characters that inspire fear have an Edge on rolls targeting their Mind defense. Frightened creatures are Exposed.

### Full Cover

If a creature is fully obscured from the vision of their attacker either behind something solid and opaque or within darkness they have Full Cover. Other creatures still know the creature is there unless they becomes Hidden.

### Grab

In order to Grab, the creature makes a Strength + Athletics roll against the target's React to give the target creature the Grabbed condition. While grabbing a target the grabbing creature can move half their speed, taking the grabbed creature along with them.

# Grabbed

The Grabbed creature's speed is reduced to 0 and their attacks have a Hindrance unless using a weapon with the Fine Tag. Grabbed creatures are Exposed. As an Action the Grabbed creature can attempt to escape by comparing their Dexterity+Athletics or Strength+Athletics to the a new Roll of the Feature that grabbed them.

# **Hard** 
Even with time and focus it requires a steady hand, extensive training, or luck. The Target Number is 12.

### Hazardous Terrain

Hazardous terrain is Difficult Terrain that also deals damage when a character begins their turn within the area or they enter the area. Steam vents, vicious brambles, or spinning blades are all Hazardous Terrain. The Hazardous Terrain may have a Target Number to avoid the damage with a React defense.

### Hidden

A creature is Hidden if they move into full cover and succeeded on Wits + Stealth check against the enemies React then move at least half their movement. The Hidden creature can move out of Full Cover and their position will not be noticed until the beginning the enemy creature's turn, or until the Hidden creature deals damage or casts a spell. Hidden creatures treat those that are unaware of them as Exposed.

### Hide

A character can attempt to become hidden by making a Wits+Stealth check while in Full Cover from their potential observers against their Mind defense. If the character is successful, the observers lose track of the character’s position in the chaos. When a Hidden character deals damage, casts a spell, or otherwise calls attention to themselves, they will be revealed. Otherwise, their position won't be noticed until the enemy creature makes a Maneuver to Scan the Battlefield.

# Hindrance
A Character rolls an additional D12 and takes the lower roll. 

# **Hover** 
Hovering creatures can move in any direction during their Turn. They are immune to being made Prone.

# HP

HP, or hit points, is an abstraction of how many times a character can be hit. Of a character drops to 0 HP they are [[Glossary#Unconscious]] and begin [[Glossary#Dying]].

# **Huge** 
Larger than sixteen feet tall, Huge creatures take up 4 by 4 spaces. They Threaten creatures within 3 spaces.

# **Impossible** 
This is the realm of gods and immortals. Only the highest tier characters can hope to succeed at these tasks. The Target Number is 24.

# **Improbable** 
Only those with mastery of a task can push themselves this far to hope to achieve this level of outcome. The Target Number is 16.

# Incapacitated

The creature cannot make an Action, Maneuver, Response, or Interaction. Incapacitated creatures are Exposed.

## Initiative

To realize a Conflict is happening, each Character needs to make a React Defense. If a specific Character initiated the Conflict it is assumed that they are aware. The Target Number is the highest React Defense of their opposing NPCs. Aware Characters can act during their side’s Turn.

## Interaction

Interactions are saying a few words, interacting with an object, drawing a weapon or item at hand, dropping an item being held, handing off an item to another willing creature, or other simple activity that doesn't require attention while distracted by combat. A creature can only make one interaction in their turn unless they use a Maneuver or Action in its place.

### Invisible

A creature is Invisible if they cannot be seen, either by a spell, ability, or in complete darkness. A creature can estimate where an invisible target is with a Wits + Senses roll using senses other than sight against the target's React. If the target is moving stealthily they can make an opposing Dexterity + Stealth roll.

# Knowledge

Knowledge is used for recalling information a creature might know and represents the training they have received.

# **Large** 
Ranging from seven feet tall to sixteen feet tall, Large creatures take up 2 by 2 spaces. They Threaten creatures within 2 spaces. 

###### Line

The spell projects forward in a line from the center of the caster's space to a space withing the Distance of the line. Every space the line crosses is within the effect.

# Line of Effect
For a feature to effect a Target, spaces need to be unobstructed emanating from the origin.

## Long Rest

Long rests are 8 hours without Conflict in which the Characters rest and do light activity like cooking, sleeping, light exercise, and reading.


## Maneuver

Maneuver's are moves that can don't rise to the level of an Action, if a creature has already used their Maneuver, they can use an Action to perform a Maneuver.

# Maximum HP

The maximum HP a creature can have. A character's [[Glossary#Base HP]] is set by their class plus their [[Glossary#Fortitude]] bonus multiplied by the character's [[Glossary#Proficiency Bonus]].

# **Medium** 
Ranging from four to seven feet tall, medium creatures take up an entire space. The Threaten 1 space unless their attack has Reach.

# Mind

[[Glossary#Aptitude]]and [[Glossary#Will]] combine to make up a creature's Mind defense. This is used to resist attempts to bend their will, confuse them, or otherwise attack the creatures psyche.

# **Moderate** 
This takes some effort to achieve, but can be achieved with time and focus. The Target Number is 8

# Moves

Inside a Conflict all Creatures have access to four Moves: Action, Maneuver, Interaction, and Response. Features will indicate what type of move they require to use.

#### Moving Through

A creature can move through another creature's space and it is considered Difficult Terrain. If a creature ends their turn within another creature's space they are Sharing a Space. Reactions by the creature who’s space is being moved through have an Edge against the moving creature. If the other creature is hostile, as a Reaction they can make a Weapon Attack against the moving creature.

# **Noble** 
Noble requires the employment of others and maintenance of a territory with regular expenditures on food, clothing, and accessories that impress the community costing 10,000 Coin per week to maintain. Social and Stealth Rolls to pass as Poor or Common individuals have a Hindrance.

### Offhand Attack

A weapon with the Fine Tag or Shield can be used to make an Offhand Attack.

#### Ongoing
If the spell is Ongoing, the Spellcasting Roll becomes the [[Target Number]] to end the spell. The target makes their Defense Roll at the end of their turn to end the spell. Every time they fail their [[Defense Rolls]] the Target Number decreases by 1.

### Pace

The Pace of the chase is set by the lead creature’s movement distance. At the beginning of a following Creature’s turn, subtract the Creature’s movement distance from the Pace and move back or forwards along the Chasemap.

# Paralyzed

The creature cannot make an Action, Maneuver, Response, Interaction, or speak. They automatically fail React and Deflect rolls. Attacks against a Paralyzed creature gain a Perk . Paralyzed creatures are Exposed.

# Perk

If you roll a 12 on the D12 – as long as you meet the Target Number – the action succeeds with an additional effect. It will always be to the benefit of the character, even if it doesn’t clearly relate to the action that prompted the Roll. Some skills, weapons, and features have default perks associated with them. If there are more than one [[Glossary#Perk]] available the Player chooses one.

#### [[Permanent]]
Permanent spells have ongoing effects that doesn't end until the caster dispels them or [Break Spell](app://obsidian.md/Break%20Spell) is cast on them.

# Petrified

Petrified creatures and their held items are changed to an inanimate substance. The creature cannot make an Action, make a Maneuver, make a Response, move, or speak. They also have Damage Resistance, and are immune to poison, disease, and aging. Poisons and diseases are not neutralized, just suspended. Petrified creatures are Exposed.

# Pilot

This is a creature's ability to control a mount, vehicle, or other conveyance.

# **Poor** 
Poor is the minimal amount a person needs to house, clothe in Common Clothes, and feed a person costs 10 Coin per week of Downtime to maintain. Social Rolls with Wealthy and Noble individuals have a Hindrance.

# Proactive Roll

When a Character seeks to achieve something the player makes a [[Glossary#Proactive Roll]]. The bonuses are a combination of a [[Glossary#Stat]], [[Glossary#Skill]], and – if the Game Maser agrees it applies – an [[Glossary#Expertise]].

# Proficiency Bonus

This is determined by the [[Glossary#Tier]] of play. [[Glossary#Tier]] 1 characters have a proficiency bonus of +1 and higher [[Glossary#Tier|Tiers]] have correspondingly higher bonuses.

# Prone

Prone creatures can only crawl using two spaces of movement for every space traveled. Standing up spends half their movement speed. The prone creature has a Hindrance on attack rolls. Prone creatures are Exposed to melee attacks.

### Pull

When pulling a target they move in the shortest path towards the controlling creature.

### Push

When pushing a target they can only be pushed directly away from the controlling creature. If the target is stopped by an immovable object the target takes 1d6 per space they didn't move. If the target is pushed through a space occupied by another creature they stop and both creatures take 1d6 damage per space the target didn't move.

###### Range

Either targets a single creature within the listed Distance, or the origin of the effect is in a square at the specific Distance.


# React

[[Glossary#Dexterity|Dexterity]] and [[Glossary#Wits]] combine to make up a creature's React defense. This is used to avoid attacks or other hazards.

# Renown
Renown provides Expertise that can be used out of combat or otherwise provide features they can use when interacting with NPCs.

# Rerolls

The Game Master may reward a Player Rerolls that they can use to give their character an Edge on a Roll of their choice.

## Response

As an immediate reaction to a specific action another creature makes during any turn. If it has a triggering Action the Response interrupts the Action and can modify it or cause it to fail. A creature has a single response they can take each round unless otherwise specified.

# Restrained

Restrained creatures total speed is reduced to 0. They have a Hindrance on their React and Deflect rolls. The restrained creature has a Hindrance on all Rolls. Restrained creatures are Exposed.

# Roll 

Roll a D12 and add bonuses and penalties then compare the total on a [[Glossary#Target Number]].

# Rolloffs

Occasionally two characters have a roll off. When they do, the lowest roll becomes the Target Number and the Perks or Complications of the winner apply.


### Round

A round has 4 phases, the Characters’ Turn, The Foe’s Turns, Lair Actions, and Environmental Events. The total time elapsed in a round is 10 seconds.

### Screened

If a creature has at least half of their body behind something solid at opaque between them and their attacker they are considered Screened and gain an Edge on defenses. Obscuring effects, like Low light, fog, or dense foliage can also Screen a creature from others more than 1 space away.

# Senses

This is a creature's awareness of the world and their surroundings.

### Sharing a Space

Two creatures can share the same space. While they are in the same space both creatures have a Hindrance on Athletics, Enigma, and Weapons rolls. As well, both creatures have a Hindrance on React and Deflect defenses. Both creatures are Exposed.

### Shift

When shifting a target the controlling creature can move them in any direction. If the target is stopped by an immovable object the target takes 1d6 per space they didn't move. If the target is shifted through a space occupied by another creature they stop and both creatures take 1d6 damage per space the target didn't shift.

## Short Rest
Taking an hour of light activity without conflict.

### Shove

A creature can Shove another creature if they're Adjacent or Sharing a Space. A creature makes a Strength + Athletics against the Body of a target no more than one size category larger to push the target up to 2 spaces away and knock them Prone.

# Sickened

Sickened creatures have a Hindrance on all Rolls.

# Simple 
Anyone with a passing familiarity with the task can achieve it. The Target Number is 4.

# Skill

A character's [[Glossary#Archetype|Archetypes]] grants a them proficiency in a number of skills. They apply their [[Glossary#Proficiency Bonus|Proficiency Bonus]] when rolling any Skills they are proficient in. The listed suggestions are just that, suggestions. The [[Glossary#Stat]] + [[Glossary#Skill]] combination should fit the task the Character is attempting.

# **Small** 
Ranging from two to four feet tall, small creatures take up an entire space. They can Move Through an occupied space without it being Difficult Terrain.

# Soak

A character's soak is their [[Glossary#Fortitude]] + the [[Glossary#Soak]] granted by their worn armor or shield. It is used to reduce the amount of damage taken, to a minimum of 1 damage.

# Soak Bonus

This bonus adds to the character's [[Glossary#Body]] to derive the character's [[Glossary#Soak]].

# Social

Social is what creatures use to interact and manipulate other creatures

### [[Spellcasting Bonus]]
To cast a spell the character needs to make a Roll with their Spellcasting Bonus, a combination of their Spellcasting Stat and their Spellcasting Skill. If there are multiple targets, the character only rolls once and applies that to all the target's defenses.

###### Sphere

The area extends around the origin like a Circle but in all directions. Sphere 0 covers the origin space.

## Spread

Unlike normal Conflicts during a Side’s Turn the participants act in the order of their position in the Spread, with the front participants acting first.

#### Standing

If a character rises from prone it uses half of the creature's Stride.

### Stabilization Roll

At the end of every Turn a Dying creature makes a 1d12 Stabilization roll against a Target Number of 6. If they succeed they gain one success, if they roll a 12 on the die, they gain an additional success. If they fail they gain one failure, and if they roll a 1 on the die, they gain an additional failure. Taking any amount of damage while Dying gives the creature on failure. After three successes they stabilize and cease to be dying at 0 HP. After three failures they die.

# Stat

Stats are the basic characteristics of a character. One of them is included in every Roll.

# Stealth

This is a creature's skill at hiding and otherwise avoiding being seen.

# Strength

Strength is the measure of brute power that a creature has. It is used for striking, lifting, jumping, and other feats of physical exertion. If a character makes an unarmed strike they do damage equal to their Strength bonus dealing a minimum of 1 damage.

# **Stride** 
Striding creatures Distance is how far they can move across level ground. Steep uphill or Difficult Terrain take twice the amount of distance to cross a single space.

# Success

Rolls meeting or beating the [[Glossary#Target Number]] – with anything other than a 1 on the D12 – succeeds.

# Suffocating

A creature can suffocate for a number of rounds equal to their Body defense. After that they begin making Stabilization Rolls at the beginning of they turn for as long as they are suffocating. Once they fail three total Stabilization Rolls they die.

# **Swim** 
Swimming creatures can move in any direction the current allows. Moving along with the current adds the current speed to the distance the creature can travel. Moving against the current subtracts that distance. They are immune to being made Prone.

### Target
The target is the area or creature that the spell effects, the range at which the spell can be cast, and the defense used to resist the spell if it is an attack. If the spell doesn't include a Defense the spell automatically succeeds. The target can choose to fail their defense.


# Target Number

# Temporary HP

Temporary HP are additional beyond the character's HP. When a character gains Temporary HP they replace the current value if the new value is higher. When the character takes damage the Temporary HP goes away first. [[Glossary#Dying]] creatures with Temporary HP are still [[Glossary#Dying]] until they stabilize. If a creature has Temporary HP they are not [[Glossary#Unconscious]] from having 0 HP.

### Threatened Space

A creature threatens spaces with their weapon. Melee weapon can threaten adjacent spaces. If the weapon has the Reach Tag it also threatens spaces listed with the Tag. Ranged weapons threaten all creatures within their Range Tag not Screened or in Full Cover.

# **Tiny** 
Tiny creatures range form six inches to two feet tall. They can share a space with any other creature without a Hinderance. Creatures can Move Through without it being Difficult Terrain. They only Threaten the space they are in.

# Tier
Advancement through the 12 levels is broken into 4 tiers. The Character's Tier limits what spells, item, feats, or other features are available to the Character. The character's [[Glossary#Proficiency Bonus]] and [[Glossary#Maximum HP]] increase on every change of Tier.

###### Touch

The Character can reach all targets they Threaten with their Natural weapon or are in an adjacent space.

## Turn

Each side acts in turn. Within their Turn there is no set order, so on their Turn, the Characters can go in the most advantageous order. Encourage your Players to strategize and order their actions to work in concert. However, each Character must make all their Moves before another Character can make their Moves.

# Unconscious

The creature cannot make an Action, Maneuver, Response, Interaction, or speak. They automatically fail React and Deflect rolls. Unconscious creatures are Exposed. They can be awakened by taking damage or another creature perform a Maneuver to awaken them.

###### Wall

The spell manifests 1 space wide, 2 spaces tall, and 6 spaces long unless otherwise stated in the description.

# **Wealthy** 
Wealthy requires the employment of others to maintain a high quality of food, clothing, and housing that can impress guests costing 1,000 Coin per week to maintain. Social and Stealth Rolls to pass among Poor individuals have a Hindrance.

### Weapon Attack

As an Action a Character makes a Weapons + Stat (as appropriate for that weapon) roll against a target's Deflect defense. If the Character succeeds, the attack connects and it deals damage according to the weapon's damage die.

A NPC declares their intent to attack a Character and the Character makes a React defense against the to the attack's Target Number.

# Weapons

This is a creature's prowess with weapons. If a weapon has multiple [[Glossary#Tag|Tags]] you can choose which [[[Glossary#Stat]] +[[Glossary#Skill]] combo to use. When dealing damage with a weapon roll, add the [[[Glossary#Stat]] bonus you used in the roll.

# Will

Will is a creature's strength of will and how well it can express it and maintain it.

# Wits

Wits are how well a creature maintains their focus in a stressful situation and how quickly they can react.
