While the Characters haven’t engaged in a Conflict they can interact with the Set Piece Features and NPCs. 

# Spotlight

The Character that is currently acting has the Spotlight. If one Player or if you the Game Master are dominating the Spotlight you should make an active effort to encouraged those who have not acted take the Spotlight.

This can be as strict as an Initiative system where a Player cannot act until all other Players have acted. Or it can be informally tracking who has acted and interjecting with prompts to other Players asking if they have an action to contribute. 

# Prolonged Moves

Prolonged Moves are those that take more time than a round of Conflict will allow. That is not to say that they cannot be done during an active Conflict, but that making Prolonged Moves during combat requires maintaining the Move with an Action each round until the Prolonged move completes. Prolonged moves take one or more minutes. If a feature provides a Prolonged move, such as Ritual Casting, it will indicate how long the Move takes.

## Breather

A Character can take a Breather by spending an hour recovering from combat. If the character make a Strength, Dexterity, or Wits Roll during the hour they do not get the benefits of the Breather.

During a Breather, making a Aptitude + Knowledge check (applying any medical Expertise) against the creature’s current HP removes all Bleeding Conditions immediately and heals 1d6 HP at the end of the Breather. As a Perk it heals 6 HP instead. As a Complication the creature gets a Blight. And as a Failure they do not heal.

# Rests

The Characters cannot purely move from conflict to conflict without a moment of respite to reset the tension. Otherwise the conflicts will blend together and lose their impact regardless of how difficult or dynamic you make it.

## Long Rest

Long rests are 8 hours without Conflict in which the Characters rest and do light activity like cooking, sleeping, light exercise, and reading.

### Healing during a Long Rest
Taking a Long Rest heals the Characters’ Body + 1d6 per Tier HP, always healing at least 1. It also removes one level of Blight of the Player’s choice. If a Healer’s Kit is used the Character heals Body + 6 HP and two levels of Blight of the Player’s Choice.

### Research
A character can use their long rest to research with a Primer by expending one charge and making an [[Glossary#Aptitude]] + [[Glossary#Knowledge]] Roll against the Tier of the Primer's Target Number. On a success the researcher gains the Research Die worth of Research Points. As a Perk they gain twice as many Research Points. As a complication you have a Hindrance on rolls when using that Primer until you succeed on the roll. On a failure the Primer doesn't have the knowledge you need and is useless for your current study, but can be used again once you spend Research Points.

### Reset Features
Many Features and Enchanted Items reset their charges on a Long Rest.

## Short Rest
Taking an hour of light activity without conflict.

### Healing during a Short Rest

During a  [[Glossary#Short Rest]] a character can make an  [[Glossary#Aptitude]] +  [[Glossary#Knowledge]] roll (applying any medical  [[Glossary#Expertise]] against the creature's current HP to heal a target 1d6  [[Glossary#HP]] and remove all  [[Glossary#Bleeding]]  [[Glossary#Conditions]], the character can only regain hp once before taking a  [[Glossary#Long Rest]].

#### Healer's Kit

Healers kit grant an  [[Glossary#Edge]] when healing damage. As an Action the healers kit can be used to end all  [[Glossary#Bleeding]] conditions.

# Downtime

When your character is not adventuring they have Downtime which is expressed in weeks. This is when they are out of Conflict, living their lives. Their actions during Downtime can trigger Conflicts which make them an excellent vehicle for discovering new Plot threads.

## Designing Downtimes

While there are a wide range of Downtimes available, not all of them will fit what the Players what their Characters to spend their time on. In this case it is on the Game Master to design the Downtime.

### Time

If the task can be completed in a week then it is best to use a Complex Roll to determine the outcome.

If the goal will take more than a week to achieve, designate the task as Research and for each Downtime designate a Complex Roll that adds points to that Research.

### Participants

Some Downtimes will support multiple characters working on the same Downtime. In this case choose a Lead for the Downtime who Rolls and allow the other participants to Assist in one Roll. Depending on the Complex Roll, different players can take on each Rolls, in that case they can either Roll or Assist.

### Difficulty

The Target Number for a Downtime is determined either by the Lifestyle of the Downtime, the Tier of the target, or the Challenge Rating of the target.

### Rewards

If the Downtime Activity has a monetary reward, the reward should generally correspond to the Target Number of the task.

### Lifestyle

Your character is accustomed to a certain Lifestyle and with it come costs. Every week of Downtime has an associated cost depending on how squalid or rich you wish to operate at.

For Downtimes that have a Target Number based on Lifestyle, the character must be of a lifestyle within one step of the Downtime's Target Number.

| Lifestyle | Cost   | TN  |
| :-------- | ------ | --- |
| Poor      | 10     | 6   |
| Common    | 100    | 8   |
| Wealthy   | 1,000  | 12  |
| Npble     | 10,000 | 16  |

- **Poor** Poor is the minimal amount a person needs to house, clothe in Common Clothes, and feed a person costs 10 Coin per week of Downtime to maintain. Social Rolls with Wealthy and Noble individuals have a Hindrance.
- **Common** Comfortable is the average lifestyle of an individual that has steady housing, easy access to food, and is able to regularly clean themselves and their clothes costing 100 Coin per week of Downtime to maintain. Social Rolls with Noble individuals have a Hindrance.
- **Wealthy** Wealthy requires the employment of others to maintain a high quality of food, clothing, and housing that can impress guests costing 1,000 Coin per week to maintain. Social and Stealth Rolls to pass among Poor individuals have a Hindrance.
- **Noble** Noble requires the employment of others and maintenance of a territory with regular expenditures on food, clothing, and accessories that impress the community costing 10,000 Coin per week to maintain. Social and Stealth Rolls to pass as Poor or Common individuals have a Hindrance.

### Settlements

Settlements can only support a certain level of lifestyle. In such settlements you cannot live a lifestyle that is greater than the settlement level. Downtimes that interact with the character's lifestyle are limited to the settlement level. Characters can maintain a lifestyle one level above the max lifestyle.

| Settlement | Max Lifestyle |
| :--------- | :------------ |
| Village    | Poor          |
| Town       | Common        |
| City       | Wealthy       |
| Metropolis | Noble         |

## Sample Downtimes

###### Carousing

To carouse a character spends an additional week Coin depending on what social class they are carousing among then performs a Complex Roll of Aptitude + Social to find the best parties; Will + Social make friends with; and a Wits + Social to be entertaining. On a success you gain a contact of that social class that can be called upon in the future. As a Perk the characters gain the "Popular" Title that grants an Edge Social Rolls with that social class and the local area until the end of their next Downtime. As a Complication gain the "Boorish" Title that grants a Hindrance when dealing with that social class in the local area until their next successful Carousing. On a Failure something goes wrong, they are arrested for a minor offense and they gain a Hindrance on their next Carousing attempt.

###### Case a Target

The characters can attempt to prepare to infiltrate a location by observing it from the outside or cultivating contacts within it with a Complex Roll against a Target Number based on the defenses of the location with a Aptitude+Senses roll to scout the location; a Wits + Senses roll to determine guard patterns or a Will + Social to get that information out of someone familiar with the location; and a Wits + Stealth to probe the defenses to validate the information gathered. On a success you get a rough map of the target location, and information on the habits of its inhabitants. As a Perk the party will not need to make checks to enter the target location. As a Complication the location will be on high alert aware someone is attempting to infiltrate the location when the party attempts to enter it. On a Failure the characters are not able to gain any information.

###### Crafting Alchemical Consumables

To craft Alchemical Items requires the Alchemical Formulae to create it, successful Aptitude + Craft Rolls as required by the Tier, and enhanced materials whose cost is based on the Tier of the item. Completing the necessary successes produces six of the consumable items.

| Tier | Target Number | Successes | Material Cost |
| :--: | :--: |:--:| :--: |
| 1 | Easy | 1 | |
| 2 | Moderate | 3 | |
| 3 | Hard | 6 | |
| 4 | Improbable | 12 | |

###### Crafting Mundane Items

To craft a Mundane item requires a successful a Moderate Aptitude+Craft Roll for every 50 Coin the item costs rolled once a Downtime. It requires an appropriately furnished workshop to work in. As a Perk the attempt counts as two successes. As a Complication the attempt damages the item and reduces the final sell price by 25 Coin per Tier. On a Failure the project doesn’t progress.

###### Crafting Enchanted Items

To craft an enchanted item requires the formulae to create it, successful Aptitude+Craft rolls, and be able to cast spells of the same Tier as the item.  It requires an appropriately furnished workshop to work in and sufficient enhanced materials to craft the item. One of those enhanced materials must be harvested from a non-beast creature thematically linked to the item of the same tier as the item.

| Tier | Target Number | Successes | Material Cost |
| :--: | :--: |:--:| :--: |
| 1 | Moderate | 1 | |
| 2 | Hard | 3 | |
| 3 | Improbable | 12 | |
| 4 | Impossible | 60 | |

###### Experimentation

As a Downtime action the researcher can make a three [[Glossary#Research Roll|Research Rolls]] against the subject's Tier's target number. The Character must be at least the Tier of the research subject. They gain the highest roll's value in [[Glossary#Research Points]]. They cannot earn research points beyond their current Tier's Max Research Points. If they have access to an appropriate facility for the task, that facility may confer an Edge on rolls or other bonuses to their experimentation.

| Tier | Target Number  |
| :--: | -------------- |
|  1   | [[Moderate]]   |
|  2   | [[Hard]]       |
|  3   | [[Improbable]] |
|  4   | [[Impossible]] |

###### Gambling

To spend a downtime gambling the character must make a Complex Roll with a Target Number based on the Lifestyle of the Character. Gambling requires risking an additional week's Lifestyle that you are Gambling among. To find a profitable game requires a Aptitude + Social roll; to strategize the game takes a Wits + Knowledge roll; and to influence the game through cheating requires a Dexterity + Stealth or through bluffing requires a Will + Social roll. On a success the character keeps their risked coin as well as winning half of what they risked. As a Perk they win half of what they risked once more. As a Complication they lose half their risked coin. And if they Fail they lose all they risked.

###### Gather Information

To find information on a person, place, or thing the characters need to make a Complex Roll against a Target Number based on how secret or obscure the information is. With a Aptitude + Social Roll to find who or where the information might be; a Dexterity + Stealth Roll to sneak in or a Will + Stealth Roll to fake their way to get the knowledge; and a Will+Social Roll to convince someone to give the knowledge or a Aptitude + Senses to extract the knowledge from documents. As a Perk the characters gain additional information. As a Complication they get caught if it is secured knowledge or learn a falsehood if it is obscure. On a failure they find nothing.

###### Pick Pockets

To take coin or treasures of associated value from targets out and about in the world requires a Hard Complex Roll. Choose the lifestyle level of your targets. To scope the targets make a Wits + Social Roll; to sneak the items off of the target make a Dexterity + Stealth Roll; and to escape undetected Dexterity + Stealth to hide or Dexterity + Social to disappear into a crowd. As a Perk you gain an item that leads to a new adventure or a treasure worth half of the take. As a Complication you get detected but escape and the targets are aware of you. If you fail you are caught and arrested by guards or attacked by your targets.

###### Pit Fighting

The Target of the Complex Roll is based on the target Lifestyle reward and can only be performed by a singular participant. Every time they Roll, the Stat in that roll is Blighted. On each roll as a Perk they gain Edge on the next roll, and as a Complication they have a Hindrance on their next roll. On a Failure, another Stat is Blighted. They fight with a Strength + Weapons roll; then a Fortitude + Athletics to endure damage or Wits + Athletics to avoid it; and Strength + Social or Dexterity + Social to work the crowd. As a Perk the character gains an additional half of the reward or gains the "Champion Fighter" Title. As a Complication the character gains a rival. On a Failure they gain the “Lost Fighter” Title.

###### Research Lore

To research the Lore of a creature or item the Target Number is based on the Tier of the target. To find the source of the information they make a Aptitude + Social roll; to gain access to the lore they make a Will+Social roll to convince the holder to to give it or a Wits + Stealth to extract it. Finally an Aptitude + Knowledge roll to interpret it, or a Will + Social to convince someone else to interpret it for you. As a Perk you gain additional information that will give you an Edge in finding or interacting with the focus of your investigation. As a Complication someone else gets the information before you, you get additional wrong information, or you fail to gain the information but learn where the information may be. On a Failure you get no information.

###### Research with a Primer

The researcher expends three charges of the Primer and make an Aptitude + Knowledge three times against the Tier of the Primer's Target Number. On a success the researcher gains 5 * the Research Die worth of Research Points. As a Perk they gain the maximum amount of Research Points. As a complication you have a Hindrance on Rolls using that Primer until you succeed using the Primer. On a failure the Primer doesn't have the knowledge you need and is useless for your current study.

	###### Primers
	Primers contain information on a specific topic and have a Research Die ranging from 1d4 at Tier 1 to 1d10 at Tier 4 depending on the Tier of the Primer, and an amount of Charges equal to 10 times the Tier. Once the researcher reaches 100 * the Primer's Tier worth of research points, that primer can no longer be used to gain new research points, until those research points are spent.

###### Creating a business

Creating a business requires an investment of 10 times the target level of Lifestyle expenses. The target Lifestyle cannot exceed the level that a settlement will support.

###### Run a Business

Running a business will provide a level of reward equal to the lifestyle the business caters to with three Aptitude + Social Rolls against the Difficulty of the lifestyle. As a Perk your income increases by half. As a Complication you only earn a half of the income. As a failure the business makes no income during that downtime.

###### Expand a Business

To increase the maximum lifestyle the business caters to requires a Complex Roll and requires spending five times the downtime expense of the target Lifestyle. A Will + Social roll gathers new clientele, if you have a patron of the target lifestyle you gain an Edge on this roll; Aptitude + Knowledge to negotiate with higher class suppliers for goods; and an Aptitude + Craft to find a better location or improve the existing one with. As a Perk it only costs half as much to expand. As a Complication it doesn’t return funds during the next Downtime activity. As a failure the expansion fails but still costs.

###### Selling Treasures

Finding someone who can take the treasure off your hands depends on the size of the settlement you are in. An Aptitude + Social to finds someone who has the funds to purchase the treasures; an Aptitude + Craft to appraise the treasure; and Wits + Social to convince them to purchase at the best price or Aptitude + Social to make the history of the treasure to highlight its value. On the success the treasures are sold at the market price. As a Perk, increase the sale price by one quarter. As a Complication the sale price is reduced by one half. On a Failure no one who can afford to buy the items are found.

###### Tracking down a magic item to purchase

To track down the current owner of a specific magical item who could be convinced to sell the party an item the characters need to make a Complex Roll against a Difficulty based on the Tier of the item. The size of the settlement you are searching determines the max Tier an item can be found in. To start they need to make an Aptitude + Knowledge to research who might have the item; an Aptitude + Social Roll to determine what the owner may want in exchange for the item; and a Will +Social roll to make the owner willing to sell the item. As a Perk the owner is willing to sell it for coin alone. With a successful roll the owner wants something in addition to coin, a favor or a lesser Tier item. As a Complication the owner will only sell in exchange for a similarly Tier item. On a failure they refuse to sell.

###### Training a Pet

Training a captured pet to rely has a Target Number of 6 plus the Beast’s Challenge. First the Character must change the beasts Demeanor towards them to Ally. They can attempt this once per Downtime by making three Will+Social Rolls. On a Success they increase the Demeanor by one rank. As a Perk it increases by two ranks. As a Complication there is no change. And on a Failure the Demeanor slips one rank.

Once the beast is an Ally, it the Beast’s Challenge worth of Downtime. At the end of each Downtime Roll a Will + Social vs 6 plus the Beast’s Challenge. On a Success gain a training. As a Perk the Downtime counts as 2 trainings. As a Complication the Downtime counts as no trainings. And a Failure removes an existent training. Between trainings, a Beast will follow its master but Hide in combat.

###### Training Pets for Combat

Training for to Attack or Help a target on command takes twice the Beast’s Challenge worth of Downtime. Make three Will + Social Rolls vs 6 plus the Beast’s Challenge. As a Perk, the Downtime counts as two trainings. As a Complication the Downtime provides no training. And on a failed roll remove two trainings.

###### Transcribing a Spell
Scribing a new spell from a scroll or other Spellbook into their Spellbook requires an [[Glossary#Aptitude]]+[[Glossary#Enigma]] roll. Scribing requires a successful [[Glossary#Downtime]] activity per [[Tier]] of the enigma. Roll against the [[Glossary#Target Number]] each [[Glossary#Downtime]] action. If the Roll fails, the pages the spell would have taken up are ruined. When copying from a Codex the character wrote the spell in, they have an [[Edge]] to copy it.

# Research
Characters can research new spells, formulae, mechanisms, even archetype abilities when not engaged in combat. This typically as Downtime Actions, but can also be done on the road as part of Long Rests. 

This is achieved by the character having a pool of "Research Points" that they can use to "buy" the knowledge of a Feature, Spell, etc.

| Subject | Points Multiplier | [[Glossary#Research Roll]] |
| :-- | :--: | : -- : |
| Alchemical Item | 10 X Tier | Aptitude+Craft |
| Archetype Feature | 100 X Tier | * |
| Enchanted Item | 100 X Tier | Aptitude+Enigmas |
| Spell | 50 X Tier | Aptitude+Enigma | 
\* Aptitude+Weapon if the class feature grants an action that makes a weapon attack or Aptitude+Knowledge otherwise.

## Primers

A core aspect of research are Primers. They can take many forms and can assist in research of topics not directly spelled out in them. For example, a wizard's codex can be a primer when researching a spell not in the codex. The notes, annotations, and formulae can act as inspiration to the character's own research. 

That is not to say Primers are universal, a wizards codex won't provide insight for a warrior researching a class Feature. Each Primer has a general topic area that it addresses, so a wizard seeking to develop a spell will need an arcane primer, whereas a warrior seeking an additional a class feature will need a martial manual, and an artificer learning a new formulae would need an alchemical or mechanical primer.

Primers contain information on a specific topic and have a Tier, a Research Die ranging from 1d4 at Tier 1 to 1d10 at Tier 4 depending on the tier of the Primer, and an amount of Charges equal to 10 times the Tier. Once the researcher reaches 100 * the Primer's Tier worth of research points, that primer can no longer be used to gain new research points, until those research points are spent.
