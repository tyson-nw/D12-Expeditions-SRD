# Alchemical Items
Alchemical Items are not made by Spellcasters, but use Enchanced Materials to power them.

## Creating Alchemical Items
Alchemical Items can be crafted by anyone familiar with the Alchemical Formula with sufficient Enhanced Materials during Downtime or Long Rests.

### Potency

> ###### Learning new Alchemical Formulas
> If you find an alchemical formula written by someone else, you can add it to your Spellbook by taking a Downtime Action to craft the item with Hinderances on all rolls.

## Purchasing Alchemical Items
Purchased alchemical item have a Potency of 4+the Target Number to create it. Purchased Alchemical Items do not have improvements. 

| Tier | Cost | Target Number |
| :--: | :--: | :--: |
|  1   | 100  | 8 |
|  2   | 500  | 10 | 
|  3   | 1000 | 12 |
|  4   | 5000 | 14 |

## Learning New Formula
> ##### Developing New Formula
> If you want to develop an Alchemical Formula you need 100 times the Tier in Research Points.

# Enchanted Items
Enchanted Items are treasures that adds [[Glossary#Feature|Features]] to a creature that drinks, wields, wears, or holds it.

## Item Descriptions

### Item Tier
Items may have an appropriate tier for characters to acquire them. These are not requirements, characters can use an item of any tier.

### Ammunition
Enchanted Ammunition breaks and cannot be recovered when expended. Thrown weapons can be enchanted as ammunition and will not break on misses.

### [[Glossary#Attuned]]
Some items require a character to attune to them, the character can only have one attuned item for every Tier of the character.

### Armor
Any type of armor can be enchanted. 

### Artifact
Artifacts are powerful items that have multiple aspects.

### Consumable
Consumable items can be used once and then they are destroyed.

### Foci
Some items can also be used as a spellcasting foci.

### Legacy
Legacy items have [[Features]] based on the Tier of the wielder. Legacy items must be [[Attuned]] to use. When gaining a new [[Feature]], it does not replace a previous [[Feature]] unless it has the same name. Though subsequent features can override some aspect of a lower Tier Feature. With the unlock of each new [[Feature]] the item needs to be re-identified at the new Tier to determine how to activate the new [[Feature]]. Most Legacy items have a base form, this can be broad encompassing a wide range of forms, or specific limiting their enchantment to a single form.

### Recharge
Some items' abilities have a limited amount of uses between Long Rests.

### Weapon
Unless otherwise noted in the description, the weapon can take any form. The item may have an listed tag that the form should have.

## Identifying an Enchanted Item

Enchanted Items have a preternatural shine to them that distinguishes them from mundane item. The investigator rolls [[Aptitude]]+[[Craft]] plus the Arcana [[Expertise]] against a [[Target Number]] to identify the item and its [[Features|Feature]].

If a creature attunes to an item before identifying its [[Features]], they can make a [[Aptitude]]+[[Focus]] toll against the [[Target Number]] to learn its [[Features]].

| Tier   | [[Target Number]] |
| :----- | :---------------- |
| Tier 1 | [[Easy]]          |
| Tier 2 | [[Moderate]]      |
| Tier 3 | [[Hard]]          |
| Tier 4 | [[Improbable]]    |

> ##### Identifying Consumables
> For consumable items the investigator can use the Alchemy Expertise and an Apothecary Pouch gives an Edge on their identification roll.

## Purchasing and Selling Enchanted Items
Other than Tier 1 consumables or Tier 0 items, Enchanted Items should not be easily purchased or sold. They are sought after treasures that provide not only prestige, but power. And most loathe to give up power. If an item is available for purchase or trade it should be done through a Downtime.

# Artifacts
Artifacts cannot be created outside of very specific and legendary events. They differentiate from lesser Enchanted Items by having multiple Features, that also have additional requirements or costs for their use. These repercussions for using the Artifact should dissuade their regular use, reserving tapping into their power for extraordinary purpose. 

## Identifying an Artifact
Determining their [[Features]] required knowledge of what artifact you are identifying or making a Hard [[Aptitude]] + [[Knowledge]] roll with any applicable History [[Glossary#Expertise]] to identify the artifact and learn its history. As a [[Perk]] the investigator also learns how to activate one [[Feature]]. As a [[Complication]] the artifact is misidentified and all subsequent rolls to investigate its aspects have a [[Hindrance]] until a successful investigation happens.  

A creature that can see magic can spend a week to make an [[Improbable]] [[Aptitude]]+[[Knowledge]] roll with the Arcana expertise to determine one [[Feature]] of the artifact. As a [[Perk]] the investigator also learns how to activate the [[Feature]] and its cost. As a [[Complication]] one aspect is misidentified and its activation deals damage, extracts additional cost, or causes a [[Complication]] every time it is activated. This continues until the investigator spends a week and makes a successful [[Improbable]] [[Aptitude]] + [[Knowledge]] roll with any applicable History [[Expertise]].
