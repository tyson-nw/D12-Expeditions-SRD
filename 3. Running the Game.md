The Game Master is the architect of encounters. They set the scene, populate it with challenges, and describe how the world changes in response to the player’s actions. But the players are the ones that decide the outcome. 

Your goal is not to tell a story, but to create set pieces where Players create the story through play.

The Game Master creates the challenges and decides on their difficulty then adjudicates the Player’s attempts to overcome the challenges. Each challenge should have a successful outcome, an advantageous outcome that gives the Characters some bonus, a successful outcome that causes a new challenge to overcome, and a failure that closes off this challenge and replaces it with a new challenge. You shouldn’t create a challenge where if the Characters fail, the game stops or nothing happens.

Keep in mind that the Game Master is also a player at the table. You should be having fun guiding the story as well as encouraging your player’s fun facing your challenges.

# Safety

Before you begin you should discuss with your players set expectations for what they can expect from your game, what sensitive topics the game may touch on, how comfortable each Player is with those topics, and how they can request a pause in game, or a reduction in description while the game is in progress. While you want to surprise your Players, you don’t want to ambush them with something that may turn them off of the game.

While this Boxed Set shouldn’t step on sensitive topics, if you plan on running a game that may include them, there are forms available to poll your Players on their comfort level with sensitive topics. 

## I need a pause.

If a player says this the Game Master should pause the game, giving the Player a minute or more to center themselves. You may want to take them aside and ask what caused the pause so you can adjust your description or the plot accordingly.

## Could you back off?

Sometimes as a Game Master you get into deep description of what is happening in game, and though it may not step on a sensitive topic, it may be more detailed then they are comfortable with. In this case, in the moment simplify the descriptions to be vaguer then when there is a break in the action take the Player aside and discuss what made them uncomfortable.

# Roleplaying

While performance is encouraged, it is not required. Funny voices, talking in character and acting as a Character can increase immersion, but they are not required for the Players and Game Master to have fun.

The Game Master can still run an engaging game just describing the world, its inhabitants, and what they say and do.

## Granting Rerolls

Be on the lookout for Players highlighting that they are playing to their Character’s Drive, Flaw, or Fear. Keep note of their Traits and provide them opportunities to incorporate them into their actions.

The core purpose a character is to have encounters and in those encounters they may come in conflict with the world. Sometimes that is other NPCs, sometimes it is an environment, and sometimes it is the Characters themselves. When that inevitable conflict occurs the game structures it in a way so all Players have an opportunity to have their Characters contribute to the resolution. When Characters enter a conflict their opportunity to act becomes structured in Rounds and turns.

# Set Pieces

A set piece is a location populated with NPCs and Features, and a set of challenges for the Characters to overcome. It is lines of dominoes set up for the players to knock over and create unexpected outcomes.

## NPCs

The world would be empty without people, so the Game Master is responsible for populating it with NPCs (Non Player Characters). They can view the Characters supportively, antagonistically,  indifferently, or anywhere between.

### Demeanor

How the NPC relates to the Characters is their Demeanor. As the players interact they may change the Demeanor up and down the ranks of the following scale.

- **Allied** They will risk themselves for the Characters. They have a -4 to Mind defense against Social Rolls.
- **Supportive** They will offer support that doesn’t put them at risk. They have a -2 to their Mind defense against Social Rolls.
- **Indifferent** They will not go out of their way to support or hinder the Characters.
- **Antagonistic** They will do what they can to hinder the Characters without putting themselves at risk. They have a +4 on their Mind defense against Social Rolls to convince them to help the Characters.
- **Hostile** They are willing to put their lives on the line to hurt the Characters. They have a +8 on their Mind Defense against Social Rolls to convince them to help the Characters.

### Traits

Just as Characters, NPCs have Traits that inform their characterization and behavior. Knowing their Drive, Flaw, and Fear will guide you in how they relate to the Characters and their actions.

# Scene Features

Every Set Piece is made up of Scene Features, they are the trappings of  the scene. Be it a dank cave dripping from stalactites to a majestic ballroom, the action doesn’t take place in a void with nothing the Characters can interact with. You should populate each Set Piece with Scene Features that the players can interact with, be it unusual Terrain, chairs that can be thrown at a foe to Expose them, tables to flip and provide cover, campfires that can be kicked to spread fire, and chandeliers to swing from are. Interacting with Scene Features should generally be a Maneuver or an Interaction.

## Visibility

Who can see what is an important aspect of a Set Piece and can add Complications or the Characters to overcome.

It is assumed that all creatures can see within areas of Bright Light and Low Light. There are other forms of vision that Creatures may have access to that negate or limit the need for light.

- **Low Light Vision** Areas of Low Light are considered Bright Light. 
- **Blindsight** Invisibility and Light are ignored within the set range.
- **Tremorsense** Invisibility of creatures touching the ground and Light are ignored within the set range.

### Full Cover

If a creature is fully obscured from the vision of their attacker either behind something solid and opaque or within darkness they have Full Cover. Other creatures still know the creature is there unless they becomes Hidden.

### Hidden

A creature is Hidden if they move into full cover and succeeded on Wits + Stealth check against the enemies React then move at least half their movement. The Hidden creature can move out of Full Cover and their position will not be noticed until the beginning the enemy creature's turn, or until the Hidden creature deals damage or casts a spell. Hidden creatures treat those that are unaware of them as Exposed.

### Invisible

A creature is Invisible if they cannot be seen, either by a spell, ability, or in complete darkness. A creature can estimate where an invisible target is with a Wits + Senses roll using senses other than sight against the target's React. If the target is moving stealthily they can make an opposing Dexterity + Stealth roll.

Even if the target is located, all attempts to hit the creature have Hindrance. Spells do not have line of effect, so the creature cannot be targeted by non-area spells.

### Screened

If a creature has at least half of their body behind something solid at opaque between them and their attacker they are considered Screened and gain an Edge on defenses. Obscuring effects, like Low light, fog, or dense foliage can also Screen a creature from others more than 1 space away.

## Light

Each light source casts an aura of bright light of a specific radius and an aura of Low Light twice the radius.

- **Daylight** Daylight is natural light from the sun. Its Dim Light is still considered daylight.
- **Bright Light** Bright light is easy to see in and confers no cover to creatures within it.
- **Dim Light** Low Light is difficult to see in. Targets in Low Light are Screened. Low light continues beyond the light source twice its spaces of Bright Light.
- **Darkness** Creatures in darkness are in Full Cover and Blinded.

### Light Sources
- **Candle**, 1 space Aura of bright light.
- **Torch**, 4 space Aura of bright light.
- **Lantern**, 6 space Aura of bright light.
- **Hooded Lantern**, 12 space Fan of bright light.
- **Daylight**, when cast through an opening the bright light fills the three times the amount of space as the opening.

## Terrain

The Terrain is the stage a Set Piece takes place on.

### Difficult Terrain

Moving through one space of Difficult Terrain requires two spaces of movement.

### Hazardous Terrain

Hazardous terrain is Difficult Terrain that also deals damage when a character begins their turn within the area or they enter the area. Steam vents, vicious brambles, or spinning blades are all Hazardous Terrain. The Hazardous Terrain may have a Target Number to avoid the damage with a React defense.

### Aquatic Terrain

- **Swimming** Unless a creature has a swim speed they move at half speed. In armor they need to make a Strength + Athletics roll with a Target Number of 6 + the armor and shield's Deflection Bonus every round or begin to sink uncontrollably 3 spaces per Round.

- **Holding Breath** A creature makes a Maneuver to hold their breath by making a Fortitude + Focus roll, they can hold it for as many rounds as a the roll. If a creature takes damage while holding their breath a Fortitude + Focus roll against the damage taken is required. If the creature fails they begin Suffocating.

### Falling

Every Round a creature can fall up to 20 spaces. For each space the creature falls that Round they take 1d6 of damage.

#### Catch Ally Response
Creatures adjacent to a falling creature can attempt to catch them as a Response with a Strength + Athletics with a Target Number of Moderate + the spaces the creature has fallen so far. If the creatures are tied together the catching creature has an Edge on catching the falling creature, but on a Failure the catching creature also begins falling.

# When should the players roll?

The Game Master should only call for a roll if the outcome of the roll would push the story forward or create an opportunity for additional roleplay. Keep in mind the characters’ Automatic Success at Simple and Easy tasks depending on their Skills and Expertises. When a player comes up with a clever solution that would be easy to pull off, or argue their point with eloquence, the Game Master can declare they succeed without a roll. If the Game Master doesn’t think their clever idea rises to the level an Automatic Success, they earn a Reroll for the effort.

When calling for a roll the Game Master should explain the consequences of a success on the roll, the consequences of a failure, and potential Complications. The potential Perk can be held back as a surprise for the players.

Players will often offer to roll to achieve a task. Before they do, the Game Master should ask what they are trying to achieve before the player rolls. The Game Master needs to know what the desired result is before they can determine the Difficulty of the action and what the appropriate Complication and Perk is before the roll. Also, the Player may have one idea of what they can achieve that contradicts what the Game Master thinks. It is best to resolve that ambiguity before the roll is made.

## Difficulty

Once the Game Master decides a Roll is necessary they set the Target Number. The Target Number should be tailored to the Difficulty of the task at hand, not the Tier of the characters. While higher Tier characters will have increased bonuses from Skills and Expertises the difficulty should stay the same based on the challenge of the task regardless of Tier. However, the difficulty should not change based on the potential consequences of a roll. Jumping over a small fissure may be Easy, but depending on the depth the consequences for failure can be dire.

- **Simple** Anyone with a passing familiarity with the task can achieve it. The Target Number is 4.
- **Easy** Anyone trained in the task can successfully achieve it. The Target Number is 6.
- **Moderate** This takes some effort to achieve, but can be achieved with time and focus. The Target Number is 8
- **Hard** Even with time and focus it requires a steady hand, extensive training, or luck. The Target Number is 12.
- **Improbable** Only those with mastery of a task can push themselves this far to hope to achieve this level of outcome. The Target Number is 16.
- **Impossible** This is the realm of gods and immortals. Only the highest tier characters can hope to succeed at these tasks. The Target Number is 24.

## Expertise

Be aware of the Character’s Expertise and allow them to apply the +2 bonus to any Roll they may be appropriate for, even if not noted in the Roll description.

## Complex Rolls

Some tasks are not achievable by a single character using a single Stat + Skill roll. In this case, the Game Master decides on three Stat + Skill rolls necessary to complete the task, and asks they players to decide which Characters will make each roll. Some of the available Stat + Skill rolls may have alternative rolls the player can make. And the players can always suggest adding an Expertise that might apply, but it is up to the Game Master to allow it. A character that has the required Skill can assist another character who is rolling, granting the rolling character an Edge on their roll. 

Rolls either succeed if they meet or beat the Complex Roll’s Target Number, or Fail if they are below the Target Number. As a Perk the roll counts as 2 successes, and Complications count as 2 failures. Once all three Rolls have been made subtract the failures from the successes. 

- **Three Successes** succeeds with a Perk.
- **Two Successes** succeed at the task.
- **One Success** succeeds at the task with a Complication.
- **No Successes** fails the task with a Complication.

## Creating Perks and Complications.

Perks and complications should be thematically tied to what the complex roll is trying to achieve. Suggested Complex Rolls will include a generic perk and complication. But depending on what rolls succeed or fail, the effect may change to fit the rolls.

### Example Complex Rolls

###### Haggling to get a deal

The party can haggle with an NPC to make a better deal than the NPC is already offering with a Complex Roll against a Target Number set by the NPC’s Mind Defense plus their Demeanor by making a Wits + Social roll to determine how much the NPC is willing to bargain; Aptitude + Knowledge to appraise the market rate of the item or task; and a Will + Social roll to convince the NPC to accept a different price. On Success the NPC will offer 10% additional in the Character’s favor. As a Perk the NPC will offer an additional 10% or something of value to the characters. As a Complication the NPC won't accept anything less than an offering of 10% in the NPC's favor. And as a Failure the NPCs demeanor drops by 1 and they refuse to make the deal.

###### Escaping through a crowd

The party can disappear into a crowd with a Moderate Complex Roll by spotting pursuers and openings with a Wits + Senses roll; leading the group from cover to cover with a Dexterity + Stealth roll or maneuver the crowd between the party and the pursuers with a Dexterity + Social roll; and moving quickly with a Strength+Athletics roll or moving nimbly with a Dexterity+Athletics roll against the React defense of their pursuers to shake them. As a Perk the party can maneuver their pursuers to the place of their choosing. As a Complication, they run into a different group of enemies. On failure the party is tracked by their enemy.

###### Navigating the Wilderness
The party can navigate from place to place and has a chance to get lost. To reach their destination they make a Complex Roll with a Target Number set by the difficulty of finding the destination with an Aptitude + Knowledge to chart the path; Wits + Senses to be aware of threats on the road; and Fortitude + Athletics to keep a pace that all party members can maintain. On a Success the party will arrive when they expect to. As a Perk they will either get there early or have an advantageous encounter as they travel. As a Complication they will have an antagonistic encounter. And on a Failure they get lost, wasting the expected travel time and have to make the Complex Roll again to find their way.

###### Tracking a creature
The party can track a creature with a Moderate Complex Roll unless the creature is moving stealthily. Then the Target Number is 6 + the creature's Wits + Stealth. They read trail signs of the target's passing with a Wits + Knowledge; spot trail signs and movement in the brush with a Wits + Senses; and either sneak on the target without giving their position away with a Dexterity + Stealth or rush to overtake them with a Strength + Athletics. On a Success they are able to catch the creature totally unaware. As a Perk, the Party can set an ambush for the creature. As a Complication a different hostile creature is stalking the party. On a failure the target slips their grasp or has lured the party into an ambush.
